Fatalities in Tuckerman Ravine

The fatalities in Tuckerman Ravine highlight just how risky a dynamic alpine environment can be. Anytime you venture into the bowl, there's a crossover into ski mountaineering because of the technicality of the terrain. Even though it’s a bucket-list destination for skiers, snowboarders, climbers, and hikers, the steep terrain and unpredictable weather have resulted in 39 recorded deaths, not all of them ski-related. The following list highlights the range of incidents that have occurred in the ravine over time.

Avalanches

  • Philip Longnecker (25) and Jacques Parysko (23) January 1954

    Buried when an avalanche struck their snow shelter beneath the headwall; Longnecker was buried, and Parysko died from hypothermia.

  • Aaron Leve (28) February 19, 1956

    Hiker buried by an avalanche in Tuckerman Ravine.

  • Scott Sandberg (32) and Thomas Burke (46) November 2002

    Climbers were killed in a multi-party avalanche incident involving seven individuals.

  • Nicholas Benedix (32) April 2019

    Killed in an avalanche in the Ravine of Raymond Cataract.

  • Karl Brushaber (37) (Ann Arbor, MI) December 24, 1974

  • Scott Whinnery (25) (Speigeltown, NY) May 8, 1976

  • Kathy Hamann (25) (Sandy Hook, CT) March 28, 1982

    Fall while climbing in Connection Gully.

  • Christopher Schneider (31) (Pittsfield, VT) March 28, 1995

  • Hillary Manion (22) (Ottawa, ON) June 3, 2001

  • Patrick Scott Powers (46) (Mansfield, MA) January 9, 2012

  • Madison Saltsburg (20) March 9, 2024
